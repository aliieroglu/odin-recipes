Home Made Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish that consists of vinegared rice served with various ingredients,
such as raw fish, seafood, vegetables, and eggs. Sushi is typically served in bite-sized pieces and
can be enjoyed either inside or outside of a seaweed wrap called nori.

Sushi is often served with condiments such as soy sauce,
wasabi, and pickled ginger, which are used to enhance the flavors of the sushi.
Sushi is enjoyed by millions of people all over the world and is known for its delicate and unique flavor combinations.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups short-grain sushi rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 4 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Nori seaweed sheets
  • Fillings of your choice (such as avocado, crab meat, cucumber, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Add the rice and water to a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice simmer for 18-20 minutes.
  4. In a separate small saucepan, heat the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until the sugar has dissolved.
  5. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and add the vinegar mixture. Stir until the mixture is evenly distributed.
  6. Place a sheet of nori seaweed onto a sushi rolling mat.
  7. Spread a layer of the rice evenly over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.
  8. Add your desired fillings onto the rice, placing them in a line near the bottom edge.
  9. Use the rolling mat to tightly roll the sushi, starting from the bottom edge and rolling upwards.
  10. Use a sharp knife to cut the roll into slices.
  11. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
