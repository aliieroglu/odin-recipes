Chicken Schnitzel

Chicken Schnitzel is a popular dish that originated in Austria and
is now enjoyed in many countries around the world. It is made by
coating thin slices of chicken breast in breadcrumbs and frying them until
they are golden brown and crispy.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Pound the chicken breasts to an even thickness using a meat mallet.
  2. Place the flour in a shallow dish.
  3. In a separate shallow dish, beat the eggs.
  4. In a third shallow dish, mix the breadcrumbs with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Coat each chicken breast in the flour, then the beaten eggs, and finally the breadcrumbs.
  6. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  7. Fry the chicken schnitzels for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  8. Remove the chicken schnitzels from the pan and drain on paper towels.
  9. Serve hot with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, steamed vegetables, or a salad.
